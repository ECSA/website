Frequently Asked Questions

The East Campus Students' Association (ECSA) is the student group for residents of East Campus Village (ECV) at the University of Alberta. The ECSA Executive consists of a President and three Vice Presidents. We host various events throughout the year and offer useful services to ECV residents. We will make sure that your experience at the university is awesome!

If you live in ECV residence, then yes! Residents of ECV include those living in the walkup buildings on 88 Ave (11008, 11016, 11024, 11031, 11035, 11039), various houses scattered throughout the ECV area, and some fraternities.

Each ECV resident pays $41 per year for ECSA association fees. $36 of this goes directly towards the ECSA budget for events and services, while the remaining $5 goes to the RHA.

ECSA House is the community centre available for ECV residents. It is located at 11036-88 Ave, and can be accessed at any time by ECV residents who have the door code. There are many awesome things in ECSA House, and various services offered during volunteer hours.

Yes! All ECV residents can book ECSA House for private events, such as a birthday party, movie night, etc. Just fill out this form, and we'll email you back with confirmation. The two main rules are that alcohol is not allowed, and you must clean up after yourself.

Feel free to fill out our Volunteer Form if you wish to get more involved! The minimum requirement as a volunteers is to open the ECSA House volunteer office for at least 2 hours a week, but there are some great perks.

If you are locked out of your room, or have any general issue or conflict, you can visit the Residence Services Office in I-House during office hours, or call the ECV Resident Assisant (RA) number at 780-619-5944 if the office is closed. Note that each resident only gets one free unlock, every other time you will have to pay $30. If you have an issue which requires immediate attention, you can again call the RAs for assistance, or campus security at 780-492-5050, or if it's a life threatening emergency, you should call 911.

If something in your unit requires maintenance, such as lights, sink, toilet, etc, you can fill out a maintenance request online.

Rent is required on or before the first day of every month that you are living in ECV. You can pay at the Residence Services Office in I-House, or you can pay online. Beware that Residence Services will charge you an extra $40 if you pay rent late!

You should make sure to keep a copy of your lease agreement that you signed when you moved into ECV. A lot of rules can be found in there, as well as the Code of Student Behaviour for all university students, and the House Rules for living in ECV.

There are a few recycle stations located in the parking lots behind the ECV walkup buildings. You can use these for garbage, recycling, and compost. You can also drop off bags of bottle/can recycling at ECSA House during volunteer office hours and the refund money will go towards more awesome events and services for ECV residents!

Each walkup building has laundry machines on the second floor, and each house will have laundry machines. Washing costs $1.25, and drying costs $1.00 and the machines accept quarters or loonies.

The East Campus Students' Association (ECSA) is only the students' association for residents of East Campus Village (ECV). However, there are other nearby residences which are collectively referred to as East Campus, including International House (I-House), Pinecrest, Tamarack, HUB, Graduate Residence, and Newton Residence.
