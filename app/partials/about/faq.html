Frequently Asked Questions

The East Campus Students' Association (ECSA) is the student group for residents of East Campus Village (ECV), Pinecrest, and Tamarack at the University of Alberta. The ECSA Executive consists of a President and five Vice Presidents. We host various events throughout the year and offer useful services to ECSA members. We will make sure that your experience at the university is awesome!

If you live in ECV residence, Pinecrest House, or Tamarack House, then yes you are! Residents of ECV include those living in the walkup buildings on 88 Ave (11008, 11016, 11024, 11031, 11035, 11039), various houses scattered throughout the ECV area, the infill houses, and some fraternities.

Each ECSA member pays $36 per year for ECSA association fees. This money goes directly towards the ECSA budget for events and services.

ECSA House is the community centre available for ECSA members. It is located at 11036-88 Ave, and can be accessed at any time by ECSA members who have the door code. There are many awesome things in ECSA House, and various services offered during volunteer hours.

Feel free to fill out our Volunteer Form if you wish to get more involved! The minimum requirement as a volunteers is to open the ECSA House volunteer office for at least 2 hours a week, but there are some great perks.

If you are locked out of your room, or have any general issue or conflict, you can visit the Residence Services Office in I-House during office hours, or call the ECV Resident Assistant (RA) number at 780-619-5944 if the office is closed. Note that each resident only gets one free unlock, every other time you will have to pay $30. If you have an issue which requires immediate attention, you can again call the RAs for assistance, or campus security at 780-492-5050, or if it's a life threatening emergency, you should call 911.

If something in your unit requires maintenance, such as lights, sink, toilet, etc, you can fill out a maintenance request online.

Rent is required on or before the first day of every month that you are living in ECV. You can pay at the Residence Services Office in I-House, or you can pay online. Beware that Residence Services will charge you an extra $40 if you pay rent late!

You should make sure to keep a copy of your lease agreement that you signed when you moved into your residence. A lot of rules can be found in there, as well as the Code of Student Behaviour for all university students, and the House Rules for living in residence.

There are a few recycle stations located in the parking lots behind the ECV walkup buildings. You can use these for garbage, recycling, and compost. You can also drop off bags of bottle/can recycling at ECSA House during volunteer office hours and the refund money will go towards more awesome events and services for ECSA members!

Each walkup building has new laundry machines on the second floor, and each house will have laundry machines. Washing costs $2.00, and drying costs $1.75 and the machines only accept payment via OneCards.

The East Campus Students' Association (ECSA) is the students' association for residents of East Campus Village (ECV), Pinecrest, and Tamarack. However, there are other nearby residences which are collectively referred to as East Campus, including International House (I-House), HUB, Graduate Residence, and Newton Residence.
